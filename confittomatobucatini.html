Bucatini with confit tomato sauce

The way you cook an item of food can transform it from bland to beautiful. Try slow roasting out-of-season tomatoes in luscious olive oil with as much garlic as you can handle to create this rich, flavoursome sauce.





Ingredients

Tomatoes on the vine: 8

Heads of garlic: 2

Olive oil: 200ml

Anchovies: 6

Bucatini or linguine: 200g

Salt and pepper to taste

Pecorino (to serve)

Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 170c
  2. Slice the tomatoes and bulbs of garlic in half at the equator. Don't worry about peeling your garlic!
  3. Put them into a baking tray where they fit snugly, and pour over the olive oil. Stir to combine and season everything to taste.
  4. When your oven is preheated, put the tray in and roast for around 45 minutes, stirring once halfway through. Everything should be soft and sizzly when it's finished.
  5. When cool enough to handle, remove a whole garlic bulb, half the tomatoes, and half the oil. You can save this for another time :-)
  6. Get your anchovies prepped! Pummel them in a pestle and mortar until they form a thick paste.
  7. When the roasted garlic is cool enough to handle, squeeze the sweet puree into the mashed anchovies and stir to combine. Mix this straight into the baking tray, mashing the tomatoes to make a richly flavoured sauce.
  8. Meanwhile, get a pot of water boiling. Once it comes to boil, add enough salt to make it taste like the sea. Add your bucatini and stir regularly to prevent it from sticking.
  9. Once al dente, use tongs to pull the bucatini directly into the baking tray. Add a ladleful of your pasta water and stir until everything comes together into a glossy, tomatoey sauce.
  10. Double check everything is seasoned to your liking, and then pile into bowls, with a good grating of pecorino and some freshly cracked black pepper.
